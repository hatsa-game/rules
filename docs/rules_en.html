Hatsa ‚Äì Game Rules

English version

1Objective of the Game

In Hatsa, players try to predict exactly how many tricks they will take each round. The more often your prediction is correct, the more points you score. The player with the highest total score wins the game.

2Scorecard and Tracking Points

Before the game starts, create a scorecard to track points for all players.

  • Write player names across the top.
  • List rounds vertically (from the lowest round up to the highest and back down).
  • After each round, record each player‚Äôs score for that round.
  • Add up all rounds at the end to determine the winner.

3Cards and Card Values

The Hatsa deck consists of multiple colours. Each colour contains cards with values 1 through 9 and E, D, C, B, A. A is the highest card. The highest card in the led suit wins the trick, unless a Hatsa card is played ‚Äì a Hatsa card always beats other colours.

4Setup

  1. Choose a dealer (for example, rock-paper-scissors).
  2. Shuffle the deck.
  3. Deal the correct number of cards per round (typically 1 up to 12 and back down).
  4. Each player receives 1 action card per round.

5Round Structure

  1. Dealing: players receive their cards.
  2. Determine Hatsa card: flip the top card of the deck; its colour becomes the Hatsa card colour for the round.
  3. Action card phase: players may use an action card (before predicting).
  4. Predicting: countdown 3-2-1 and reveal predictions using fingers.
  5. Playing tricks: follow suit if possible; the highest card in the led suit or the highest Hatsa card wins.
  6. Make your prediction count: use your high cards and Hatsa cards to win the number of tricks you predicted. Pull it off? Then you score points!

6Action Cards

Each player receives one random action card. Action cards are single-use special abilities that may only be used before predicting.

During the action card phase:

  • The player to the dealer‚Äôs left goes first.
  • In clockwise order, players decide whether to use their action card or keep it.
  • Using an action card is optional; if it doesn‚Äôt help your hand, you can save it.
  • When an action card is used, resolve the effect immediately and remove it from the game.
The effect of each action card is written on the card itself.

7How to Predict

Analyse your hand based on high cards, Hatsa cards, and distribution across suits. Decide how many tricks you realistically expect to take and how much risk you want to take.

8Scoring

Correct prediction: 10 points + 5 points per trick taken.

Incorrect prediction: no bonus and -5 points per trick difference.

Examples:

  • Predicted 3, took 2 ‚Üí -5 points
  • Predicted 5, took 2 ‚Üí -15 points

9End of the Game

After the final round, all players add up their points. The player with the highest total score wins.
